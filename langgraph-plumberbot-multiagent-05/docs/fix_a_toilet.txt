How to Fix Common Toilet Problems
==================================

Most toilet problems are caused by a handful of simple mechanical failures inside the tank. Understanding what each part does makes diagnosis straightforward.

TANK COMPONENTS OVERVIEW
--------------------------
- Fill valve (ballcock): refills the tank after each flush
- Flapper: rubber seal that opens to flush and closes to hold water in the tank
- Float: tells the fill valve when the tank is full
- Flush handle and chain: lifts the flapper when you flush
- Overflow tube: prevents the tank from overflowing by draining excess water

RUNNING TOILET (WATER CONSTANTLY TRICKLING)
---------------------------------------------
The most common cause is a faulty flapper or a float set too high.

Test 1 — Flapper leak:
1. Add a few drops of food coloring to the tank (do not flush).
2. Wait 10 minutes.
3. If color appears in the bowl without flushing, the flapper is leaking.
Fix: Turn off the water supply valve. Flush to empty the tank. Unhook the old flapper from the two side pegs and detach the chain. Take the old flapper to a hardware store to match the size and material. Install the new flapper and reconnect the chain (leave 1/2 inch of slack).

Test 2 — Float too high:
1. Look inside the tank. If water is spilling into the overflow tube, the float is set too high.
Fix: Bend the float arm downward slightly (older ball-float style), or turn the adjustment screw on a cup-float to lower the water level. The water line should sit about 1 inch below the top of the overflow tube.

WEAK OR INCOMPLETE FLUSH
--------------------------
Causes: clogged rim jets, low water level, or a worn flapper that closes too quickly.

Fix — Adjust water level: The tank should fill to about 1 inch below the overflow tube. Adjust the float upward to raise the water level.

Fix — Clean rim jets: Mineral deposits can clog the small holes under the toilet rim. Use a small wire (like a bent coat hanger) or a dental pick to clear them. Pour white vinegar into the overflow tube and let sit for an hour to dissolve deposits.

Fix — Adjust the chain length: If the chain is too long, it can get caught under the flapper and prevent a complete seal. Shorten it so there is only about 1/2 inch of slack.

TOILET NOT FLUSHING AT ALL
----------------------------
1. Check if the handle is broken or disconnected from the chain.
2. Make sure the water supply valve behind the toilet is fully open.
3. Check if the chain has come unhooked from the flapper.

CLOGGED TOILET
---------------
Method 1 — Plunger:
1. Use a flange plunger (cup-shaped, not flat). Insert it so the flange goes into the drain hole.
2. Push down slowly to expel air, then pull back sharply to create suction.
3. Repeat 10–15 times with vigorous pumping.
4. Flush to test. Repeat if needed.

Method 2 — Toilet auger (closet snake):
1. Insert the rubber-coated cable into the bowl opening.
2. Crank the handle clockwise while pushing gently.
3. When the blockage clears, pull the snake back slowly (rotating as you go).
4. Flush to clear.

Never use chemical drain cleaners in a toilet — they can crack the porcelain and damage seals.

TOILET ROCKING OR LEAKING AT THE BASE
---------------------------------------
Water or a bad smell at the base indicates a failed wax ring seal.
This is a moderately advanced repair:
1. Turn off the water supply and flush to empty the tank.
2. Disconnect the supply line and unbolts from the floor bolts.
3. Lift the toilet off and set aside.
4. Scrape off the old wax ring from both the flange and the toilet horn.
5. Press a new wax ring onto the toilet horn, then lower the toilet onto the flange bolts.
6. Press down firmly, bolt down, reconnect supply, and test.
Recommendation: If the flange is cracked or the floor is damaged, call a plumber.

WHEN TO CALL A PROFESSIONAL
-----------------------------
- Water is leaking from the tank-to-bowl connection (bolts or gasket failure).
- The toilet flushes slowly and drain cleaning hasn't helped — could be a venting issue.
- Multiple toilets or drains are backing up simultaneously.
- You see sewage or smell sewer gas — evacuate and call immediately.

MAINTENANCE TIPS
-----------------
- Never flush wipes (even "flushable" ones), paper towels, cotton balls, or dental floss.
- Check the flapper annually — they degrade over time and cause hidden water waste.
- A running toilet can waste 200 gallons per day. Fix it promptly.
