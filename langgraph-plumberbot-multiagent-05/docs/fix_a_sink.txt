How to Fix Common Sink Problems
================================

A leaky or slow-draining sink is one of the most common household plumbing issues. Most problems can be diagnosed and fixed without calling a plumber if you have basic tools and a little patience.

LEAKY FAUCET (DRIPPING WHEN OFF)
----------------------------------
A dripping faucet is usually caused by a worn washer or O-ring inside the faucet handle.

Tools needed: adjustable wrench, screwdrivers, replacement washers or cartridge.

Steps:
1. Turn off the water supply valves under the sink (turn clockwise to close).
2. Turn on the faucet to release any remaining pressure.
3. Remove the faucet handle — usually held by a screw under a decorative cap.
4. Inspect the stem: look for a worn rubber washer at the bottom (compression faucets) or a cracked cartridge (cartridge faucets).
5. Replace the washer or cartridge with an identical part from your hardware store.
6. Reassemble in reverse order and turn the water back on slowly.
7. If the leak continues, the valve seat may be corroded and needs replacement — call a plumber.

SLOW DRAINING SINK
-------------------
A slow drain is almost always caused by a buildup of hair, soap scum, or grease in the P-trap or drain.

Method 1 — Baking Soda and Vinegar:
1. Pour 1/2 cup of baking soda into the drain.
2. Follow with 1/2 cup of white vinegar.
3. Cover the drain and wait 15 minutes.
4. Flush with a full kettle of boiling water.
5. Repeat if needed. Good for mild clogs and maintenance.

Method 2 — Clean the P-Trap:
1. Place a bucket under the curved pipe (P-trap) beneath the sink.
2. Unscrew the slip-joint nuts by hand or with pliers.
3. Remove the P-trap and clean out any debris.
4. Check the pipes going into the wall for buildup.
5. Reassemble and run water to test.

Method 3 — Drain Snake:
1. Insert a hand-cranked drain snake into the drain opening.
2. Crank it clockwise as you push it deeper.
3. When you feel resistance, crank to break up or pull out the clog.
4. Run hot water to flush the drain.

LEAKING UNDER THE SINK
------------------------
If water is pooling under your sink, check:
- P-trap connections: Tighten slip-joint nuts by hand (do not overtighten plastic fittings).
- Drain basket: The rubber gasket around the drain basket may be deteriorated. Replace with plumber's putty.
- Supply lines: Check the braided lines running from the shut-off valves to the faucet. Replace if cracked or wet.
- Faucet base: If water pools around the base, the O-rings inside the faucet body need replacement.

WHEN TO CALL A PROFESSIONAL
-----------------------------
- Water is discolored or smells unusual (could indicate pipe corrosion or contamination).
- You see water stains in the cabinet below suggesting a slow hidden leak.
- Drains back up in multiple fixtures at once (indicates a main line problem).
- The shut-off valves under the sink won't close fully.

PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE
-----------------------
- Use a drain strainer to catch hair and debris.
- Flush drains monthly with boiling water.
- Never pour grease, coffee grounds, or harsh chemicals down the drain.
- Inspect under-sink supply lines every year and replace every 5–7 years.
