What to Do During a Burst Pipe Emergency
==========================================

A burst pipe is one of the most damaging plumbing emergencies. Acting quickly in the first few minutes dramatically reduces water damage to your home. Stay calm and follow these steps.

IMMEDIATE ACTION — FIRST 2 MINUTES
-------------------------------------

Step 1: Shut off the main water supply
Find your home's main shut-off valve and turn it off immediately. Water will stop flowing to all fixtures.

Where to find it:
- Basement or utility room: look for a pipe coming through the foundation wall with a valve on it
- Crawl space: near where the main line enters the house
- Outside: near the foundation (sometimes in a metal box flush with the ground)
- In warm climates: often on an exterior wall near the front of the house
- Apartment/condo: contact your building manager; there may be a per-unit shut-off in a utility closet

Turn the valve clockwise (or flip a ball valve lever to perpendicular with the pipe) to stop flow.

Step 2: Turn off the water heater
After shutting off the main, turn off your water heater (gas: set to PILOT; electric: flip the breaker). Running a water heater without water can damage the unit.

Step 3: Open cold taps
Open all cold water faucets to drain remaining water from the pipes and relieve any remaining pressure. This minimizes how much water ends up inside your walls or floors.

Step 4: Turn off electricity in affected areas
If water is near electrical outlets, switches, or the breaker box, switch off power to those areas at the breaker panel. Do not enter a flooded area if there is any chance of electrical contact.

CONTAIN THE DAMAGE
-------------------
- Place buckets and towels under the leak to catch water.
- Move furniture, electronics, rugs, and valuables out of the wet area.
- If water is coming through the ceiling, place a bucket and consider puncturing the lowest point of a water-filled ceiling bubble to let water drain in a controlled way (rather than having it collapse).
- Take photos of all damage for insurance purposes before cleanup begins.

CALL FOR HELP
--------------
After shutting off the water, call PlumberBot for emergency dispatch. Have ready:
- Your address
- Your phone number (for account lookup)
- Which pipe burst (location in the house)
- Whether water has reached electrical systems
- Whether water has stopped flowing (confirms shut-off worked)

Our on-call plumber is available 24/7 for burst pipe emergencies. Response time: typically 1–2 hours.

TEMPORARY FIXES (WHILE WAITING FOR THE PLUMBER)
------------------------------------------------
These are NOT permanent fixes — they buy time until professional repair:

Pipe repair clamp: A rubber sleeve with metal clamps, available at hardware stores. Slide over the burst section and tighten.

Pipe repair tape (self-fusing silicone tape): Dry the pipe surface first. Wrap tightly around the damage, stretching as you go. Works on small splits — not on joints or fittings.

Rubber patch and hose clamp: Cut a piece of rubber (garden hose works), place over the crack, and secure with two hose clamps on either side.

Important: These fixes can fail under water pressure. Turn the water back on slowly and only to the minimum pressure needed. Keep monitoring.

AFTER THE EMERGENCY
--------------------
1. Document everything with photos and video for your insurance claim.
2. Contact your homeowners or renters insurance as soon as possible.
3. Begin drying out the affected area: open windows, run fans, use a dehumidifier. Mold can begin growing within 24–48 hours in wet drywall.
4. Have the plumber assess whether other pipes may be at risk (common in freezing weather).

PREVENTING FROZEN PIPES IN WINTER
------------------------------------
Frozen pipes are the leading cause of burst pipes:
- Keep interior temperature above 55°F even when away.
- Open cabinet doors under sinks on exterior walls to let warm air reach pipes.
- Let faucets drip slightly during extremely cold nights.
- Insulate pipes in unheated areas (attic, crawl space, garage) with foam pipe insulation.
- Know where your main shut-off is before winter arrives.

WHEN TO CALL IMMEDIATELY (DO NOT WAIT)
-----------------------------------------
- You can smell gas along with the water leak (leave the house, call your gas company from outside)
- Water is rising rapidly and you cannot reach the shut-off
- Water has contacted electrical panels or outlets
- Sewage (dark, smelly water) is backing up into your home — this is a health hazard
